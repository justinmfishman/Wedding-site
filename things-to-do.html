Activities

North Shore / Hale'iwa — Cute local shops, garlic shrimp, surf beaches (Pipeline, Laniakea for turtles).
Nu'uanu Pali Lookout — View of the Windward Side, historical battle site.
Sky Diving — Take off from North Shore. skydivehawaii.com
Shark Diving — Swim with wild sharks without a cage! oneoceandiving.com
Iolani Palace — The only royal palace in the US. iolanipalace.org
Bishop Museum — History, traditions & geology of Hawai'i. bishopmuseum.org
Snorkeling at Hanauma Bay — Book in advance!
Pearl Harbor — Working naval base, book ahead.
Toa Lu'au — Native Hawai'ian-owned lu'au in a botanical garden. toaluau.com

Beaches

Waikiki Beach — Great for surfing lessons.
Waimanalo / Sherwood Beach — Quieter, big swimmable waves.
Kailua Beach — Our local beach!
Lanikai Beach — Small but beautiful, twin islands offshore.
Laniakea Beach — Best chance of seeing a turtle.

Hiking

Lanikai Pillbox — Short steep hike, incredible views, great at sunrise.
Maunawili Ditch & Creek Loop — Tropical, jungle scenery.
Judd Trail — Cool trees, swimmable watering hole.
Manoa Falls Trail — Starts in residential Honolulu.

Food & Drink

Poke: Ali'i Fish Market, Paradise Poke

Kava: 'Awa Hou! (Honolulu), Kava Queen (North Shore)

North Shore Shrimp: Jenny's Shrimp Truck (#1), Big Wave Shrimp Truck (Guy Fieri's #1), Da Seafood Cartel (shrimp ceviche)

Shave Ice: Island Snow, Waiola Shave Ice

Coffee: Drip Studio (Chinatown), The Bird's Nest (Hale'iwa), Coffee Lab by Hala Tree Cafe

Upscale Local Fusion: Fete (farm-to-table in Chinatown), Giovedi (Italian-Asian fusion), Arden (former Mak & Ming owners)

Hawai'ian Food: Helena's, Waiahole Poi Factory

Breakfast/Brunch: Koko Head Cafe (Kaimuki), Over Easy (Kailua)

Tiki Bars: Skull & Crown Trading Co. (Chinatown), La Mariana Sailing Club, Arnold's Beach Bar (Waikiki)
